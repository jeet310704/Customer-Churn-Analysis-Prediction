CUSTOMER CHURN ANALYSIS DASHBOARD
Phase 5: Tableau Dashboard Summary
============================================================

OVERVIEW
--------
This dashboard visualises customer churn patterns across a 700-customer
dataset. It is designed to help business stakeholders answer key
retention questions without needing to query raw data directly.

Data source: tableau/customer_churn_tableau.csv (17 columns, 700 rows)
Prepared by: scripts/prepare_tableau_data.py


WHAT THE DASHBOARD SHOWS
-------------------------
1. Overall churn rate and key customer metrics (KPI row)
2. Which contract types, payment methods, and internet services
   are associated with the highest churn rates
3. How churn varies across customer age and tenure groups
4. Which customers are High Risk, Medium Risk, or Low Risk
5. The relationship between monthly charges, support calls, and churn
6. A drillable table of the highest-risk customer segment


MAIN BUSINESS QUESTIONS ANSWERED
----------------------------------
Q1. What percentage of our customers are churning?
    -> See KPI card: Churn Rate

Q2. Are Month-to-Month customers more likely to churn than
    customers on longer contracts?
    -> See: Churn Rate by Contract Type (Row 2)

Q3. Does payment method affect churn behaviour?
    -> See: Churn Rate by Payment Method (Row 2)

Q4. Are newer customers (low tenure) at higher risk?
    -> See: Churn Rate by Tenure Group (Row 3)

Q5. Which customer profile represents the highest churn risk?
    -> See: Churn Rate by Risk Level + High-Risk Customer Table (Row 3-4)

Q6. Do high monthly charges or frequent support calls predict churn?
    -> See: Avg Monthly Charges by Churn + Support Calls vs Churn (Row 4)


RECOMMENDED DASHBOARD LAYOUT
------------------------------
Row 1 (Top):    6 KPI cards - Total Customers, Churned, Churn Rate,
                Avg Monthly Charges, Avg Tenure, Avg Support Calls

Row 2 (Middle): 3 bar charts - Churn Rate by Contract Type,
                Payment Method, Internet Service

Row 3:          3 bar charts - Churn Rate by Tenure Group,
                Age Group, Customer Risk Level

Row 4 (Bottom): Avg Monthly Charges by Churn, Support Calls vs Churn,
                High-Risk Customer Segment Table

Filters (Side/Bottom):
                Gender, Contract Type, Payment Method, Internet Service,
                Age Group, Tenure Group, Customer Risk Level


DASHBOARD-FRIENDLY COLUMNS ADDED
----------------------------------
Column                  Description
---------               -----------
churn_numeric           1 = churned, 0 = retained (enables AVG = churn rate)
age_group               Age buckets: 18-25, 26-35, 36-45, 46-55, 56+
tenure_group            Tenure buckets: 0-12, 13-24, 25-48, 49+ Months
monthly_charge_group    Low (<$45), Medium ($45-$80), High (>$80)
support_call_group      0-1 Calls, 2-3 Calls, 4+ Calls
customer_risk_level     High / Medium / Low Risk (scored on 5 risk factors)


RISK SCORING LOGIC (customer_risk_level)
-----------------------------------------
Each customer is scored 0-5 based on known churn risk factors:
  +1  Month-to-Month contract
  +1  Electronic Check payment method
  +1  Monthly charges above $70
  +1  4 or more support calls
  +1  Tenure of 12 months or fewer

  Score 3-5  =  High Risk
  Score 1-2  =  Medium Risk
  Score 0    =  Low Risk


KEY INSIGHTS THE DASHBOARD SHOULD REVEAL
------------------------------------------
1. Overall churn rate is approximately 54% in this dataset.

2. Month-to-Month customers churn at ~65% vs ~34% for Two Year
   contract holders. Encouraging contract upgrades is a direct
   retention lever.

3. Electronic Check customers churn at ~66%, the highest of any
   payment method. Offering auto-pay incentives could reduce churn.

4. Customers in the 0-12 Month tenure group churn at the highest
   rate, confirming that the first year is the critical retention window.

5. High Risk customers churn at a significantly higher rate than
   Medium or Low Risk customers, validating the risk scoring model.

6. Churned customers pay on average ~$6 more per month than retained
   customers, suggesting that high bills contribute to dissatisfaction.

7. Customers who made 4 or more support calls are disproportionately
   represented in the churned group.
